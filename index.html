Dungeon Master Mate — a mobile-first encounter tracker for tabletop RPGs.

Rule System
Each system keeps its own separate NPC library, ailments, players, and encounters — switching will not affect the other system's saved data.

1. Players — Go to the Players tab and add each player character by name. They will appear in the + Player dropdown inside every encounter.

2. NPC Library — Over 300 SRD monsters load automatically from the Open5e bestiary the first time you open the NPC tab. Each monster comes with its real stat block: HP, AC, Perception, CR, XP, attack bonus, and all of its attacks with damage dice, bonuses, and damage types. Tap 🔄 Refresh anytime to re-fetch. Use the search box to filter 300+ monsters instantly. Custom NPCs can be added manually — pick a CR and the app calculates HP, AC, and XP automatically using official Monster Manual averages.

3. Ailment Library — Create reusable status effects with a name, duration in rounds, and damage dice (No Damage through 2d10). Default D&D 5e conditions are pre-loaded on first launch.

4. Settings — Export a full backup of your library to a JSON file and import it on any device to restore your data.

Creating an Encounter — Tap + New Encounter on the Encounters tab. Use 🔍 + NPC to search and add monsters (added instantly with real bestiary stats and a d20 initiative roll), and + Player to add party members (enter their initiative manually, tap ✎ to edit).

⚖️ Scale Rating — Every encounter has a collapsible Scale Rating panel showing a live difficulty badge (Trivial/Easy/Medium/Hard/Deadly) computed using the official DMG formula — total monster XP × the real encounter multiplier, compared against XP thresholds for your party's size and level. Set Party Size and Party Level, then use the -25% / -10% / +10% / +25% Quick Adjust buttons to scale every NPC's HP, attack damage, and XP up or down on the fly. Scaling compounds — repeated adjustments stack correctly, and the encounter's XP total header always reflects the current scaled value.

🔄 Begin Round / Turn Cycling — Tap 🔄 Begin Round 1 to start the initiative cycle. The combatant whose turn it is gets a glowing gold border and a "CURRENT TURN" badge, and automatically sorts to the top of the list. A status bar shows the current round number and how many combatants have acted.

▶ Start Turn — Resets that combatant's action count back to 3 fresh actions for the turn. Does not touch ailments or cooldowns.

🏃 Move / 🎲 Roll to Hit — Each NPC card has a Move button (uses one action, no roll) and a Roll to Hit button showing the current attack number and any multi-attack penalty (Attack 1 → Attack 2 (-5) → Attack 3 (-10), capped at 3 total actions per turn). Move does NOT add to the attack penalty — only actual attack rolls do.

Hit / Crit / Miss — Tapping Roll to Hit shows an inline banner with the d20 roll and modifier total, plus three buttons: ✅ Hit, 💥 Crit (doubles damage), and ❌ Miss. All three consume one attack normally. Choosing Hit or Crit reveals the attack list; picking an attack shows the damage result inline on the card with a Dismiss button.

Hit Points — Colour-coded HP bar (green → yellow → red). Use -1 / -5 / -10 to apply damage, or toggle 🗡 Dmg / 💚 Heal to switch to +1 / +5 / +10 healing.

Cooldowns (CD) — Each attack has a CD dropdown (1–6 rounds). Once set, it becomes a red ⏳ countdown that ticks down as the encounter progresses via End Turn.

Ailments — Assign status effects from your library to any combatant. Multiple ailments can stack, each with an independent round counter.

⏹ End Turn — Applies ailment damage, ticks down ailment rounds and cooldowns, then automatically advances to the next combatant in initiative order. If a combatant dies mid-turn from any cause, their turn auto-completes and skips to the next.

Death, Skip & Revive — At 0 HP, an NPC shows a DEAD overlay with a 💚 Revive (25% HP) button. Defeated NPCs are automatically skipped when it would be their turn in the cycle.

Auto-Numbering — Adding multiple NPCs of the same type automatically numbers them ("Goblin 1", "Goblin 2"...) so they're easy to tell apart.

XP Tracking — The encounter header shows earned XP vs total XP, turning green once every NPC is defeated. If the encounter has been scaled, this total reflects the scaled value.

Bestiary — First visit to the NPC tab fetches all SRD monsters from Open5e and caches them locally; later visits load instantly. Tap 🔄 Refresh to force a re-fetch.

Search — Filter all 300+ monsters by name in real time, showing CR and attack count per result.

Custom NPCs — Build homebrew monsters with a name, CR, resistance, boss flag, and individual attacks (name, dice, bonus, damage type). HP, AC, Perception, and XP are derived from the chosen CR using Monster Manual averages.

Recharge Attacks — Attacks with a Recharge value (e.g. Acid Breath Recharge 5–6) are parsed automatically; manage the actual cooldown countdown per-encounter with the CD dropdown.

Creating Ailments — Name, duration (1–3 rounds), and damage: No Damage, 1d4 through 1d12, or 2d4 through 2d10.

Applying Ailments — Assign from any combatant card. Multiple ailments stack independently on the same target.

End Turn Processing — Tapping ⏹ End Turn rolls each active ailment's damage, subtracts it from HP, and decrements its round counter, removing expired ones automatically.

Defaults — All official 5e conditions (Blinded, Charmed, Frightened, Paralyzed, Stunned, etc.) plus common damaging effects (Poison, Bleeding, Frostbite, etc.) are pre-loaded on first launch.
